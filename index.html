Timeline

  • 1934

    Earned a PhD in mathematics from Yale University.

  • 1944

    After enlisting in the Navy in World War II, Grace became one of four principal programmers of the Mark I computer at Harvard.

  • 1949

    Joined the team devloping UNIVAC, the first commercial computer produced in the United States.

  • 1952

    Invented the first compiler, paving the way for new high-level language development.

    "Nobody believed that. I had a running compiler and nobody would touch it. They told me computers could only do arithmetic."

    The Wit and Wisdom of Grace Hopper

  • 1959

    As a technical consultant to the Conference on Data Systems Languages, she helped define the pivotal COBOL business language.

  • 1967

    Appointed Director of the Navy Programming Languages Group.

  • 1983

    Promoted to Commodore by President Ronald Reagan. (the O-7 designation later became known as Rear Admiral)

  • 1992

    Grace died at the age of 85, and was buried at Arlington with full military honors.

  • 1996

    The Navy launches the USS Hopper, Arleigh Burke class destroyer. It was only the second U.S. Navy warship to be named after a female Navy officer.
